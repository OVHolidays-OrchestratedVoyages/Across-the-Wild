Welcome to the Wild

Sri Lanka’s wild heart beats across diverse landscapes — ancient forests, vast savannas, montane highlands, and untamed coastlines. This 14-day expedition immerses you in the island’s greatest wildlife encounters, from witnessing elephant gatherings and leopard sightings to walking with indigenous communities and paddling through serene waterways.

✦

Whether you’re cycling through Sigiriya’s forest trails, safari-ing across Yala’s legendary leopard kingdom, or unwinding on Tangalle’s pristine beaches, each day reveals the raw, untamed beauty that makes Sri Lanka a sanctuary for nature lovers and wildlife adventurers.

Days 02–03 · Cultural Triangle

Habarana — Wildlife & Village Life

BD

Day 02: After breakfast, check out and depart for Habarana.

Travel: Kotugoda — Habarana | 149 km | Approx. 3 hours

Check in at the hotel. At 14:30, depart for a jeep safari at Minneriya or Kaudulla National Park. Depending on the season, you’ll encounter herds of elephants gathering by ancient reservoirs — a truly unforgettable spectacle. With scrub forest, open grassland, and shimmering water as your backdrop, watch as elephants emerge to feed, socialise, and bathe at the water’s edge as the sun dips low. Approx. 3 hours.

Day 03: Sigiriya Forest Bike Safari — Start: 07:30, Duration: approx. 3 hours. Cycle through Sigiriya Kaley forest trails, home to rich birdlife, monkeys, and other wildlife. Your guide will uncover local myths, wildlife tracking techniques, and natural history as you ride past ancient lakes and village stops.

Optional: Kayak Safari on Kala Weva — Start: 16:30, Duration: 2 hours. Glide through the waterways of Kala Weva, spotting herons, kingfishers, and the occasional mugger crocodile. Life jackets and safety gear provided.

Days 04–05 · Eastern Wilderness

Gal Oya — Sri Lanka’s Secret Wild

BLD

Day 04: After breakfast, check out and drive to Gal Oya.

Travel: Habarana — Gal Oya | 183 km | Approx. 3 hours 45 minutes

Arrive and check in at the extraordinary Gal Oya Lodge — nine bungalows set across 20 acres of private forest, constructed from locally sourced natural materials, with a restaurant, bar, and pool overlooking a backdrop of low-lying mountains.

This afternoon, embark on the Gal Oya Boat Safari — the only water-based wildlife experience in Sri Lanka. Cruise across the vast Senanayake Samudraya reservoir, where elephants swim between islands, crocodiles bask on rocky shores, and rare endemic birds take flight at dusk. Approx. 4 hours.

Day 05: Jungle Drive & Walk Through Gal Oya NP — Drive into the remote Nilgala section of Gal Oya National Park — once the sacred hunting ground of ancient royalty, rich with endemic wildlife and medicinal flora. Search for herds of elephants, four species of deer, and a remarkable variety of endemic birds.

Walk with the Chief Veddha — Duration: approx. 2 hours. Gal Oya Lodge neighbours one of the last remaining communities of the Veddha, Sri Lanka’s indigenous forest people. The village Chief leads you through the jungle, sharing his tribe’s use of medicinal plants, ancient hunting grounds, and cave dwellings.

Days 06–07 · Highland Wilderness

Nuwara Eliya — Mist, Mountains & Leopards

BD

Day 06: After breakfast, check out and journey to Nuwara Eliya, Sri Lanka’s cool, mist-laden highland capital.

Travel: Gal Oya — Nuwara Eliya | 121 km | Approx. 3 hours

Arrive and settle in at Goatfell — a characterful highland bungalow surrounded by colonial-era gardens. Evening at leisure.

Day 07: Depart early with a packed breakfast for Horton Plains National Park — a UNESCO World Heritage Site at over 7,000 feet above sea level.

Travel: Nuwara Eliya — Horton Plains | 30 km | Approx. 1.5 hours | Excursion: approx. 4 hours

Trek across frost-kissed grasslands past Baker’s Falls and to the edge of World’s End — a breathtaking 3,700-foot sheer drop. The plains shelter sambar deer, fishing cats, otters, wild boar, and if fortune favours you, the elusive Sri Lankan Leopard padding silently through the mist.

Days 08–10 · Wild South

Yala — The Leopard Kingdom

AI

Day 08: After breakfast, check out and journey south to Yala.

Travel: Nuwara Eliya — Yala | 125 km | Approx. 4 hours 20 minutes

Check in at Uga Chena Huts. At 14:30, depart for an afternoon jeep safari through Yala National Park — 1,259 sq km of grassland, scrub forest, and coastline sheltering the world’s highest density of wild leopards, alongside elephants, sloth bears, and a spectacular variety of birdlife. Approx. 4 hours.

Day 09: Morning Bush Walk — Start: 06:00, Duration: 1.5 hours. Walk Yala’s wild coastline at dawn with expert rangers — through dramatic dunes, rocky outcrops, and untouched beach where sea turtles come to nest.

Afternoon Yala Block 5 Safari — Explore the exclusive, rarely-visited Block 5 — 6,600 hectares of unspoiled wilderness and serene reservoir. An intimate encounter with Sri Lanka’s wildest heart.

Day 10: Early wake-up at 04:45 for a dawn jeep safari in Bundala National Park — Sri Lanka’s first Ramsar wetland and a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve. A vibrant mosaic of lagoons, salt pans, and scrub forest, home to nearly 200 bird species including the iconic greater flamingo. Approx. 4 hours.

Optional: Sithulpawwa Raja Maha Viharaya — Visit this ancient 2nd century BC Buddhist monastery tucked into rocky outcrops within Yala National Park. A serene excursion with sweeping jungle views. Approx. 2 hours.

Days 11–13 · Coastal Retreat

Tangalle — Sea, Stillness & Southern Shore

B

Day 11: After a leisurely breakfast, check out and drive to Tangalle for a relaxed beach stay.

Travel: Yala — Tangalle | 146 km | Approx. 50 minutes

Check in at Anantara Peace Haven. The afternoon is entirely at leisure.

Days 12–13: Two full days of complete freedom. The beach, the pool, and the rhythm of the southern ocean are yours.

Optional: Snorkelling in Tangalle — Discover vibrant coral gardens and colourful reef fish in the calm, crystal-clear waters along the southern coast. Gentle enough for all experience levels.

Optional: Kayaking in Rekawa Lagoon — Glide through mangrove-fringed waterways where herons, kingfishers, and water monitors keep quiet company. Best at dawn or late afternoon as the light softens.

Before You Go

Important Notes

Poya Days
Alcohol service is restricted on religious holidays (Poya Days). Alcohol is not available for purchase at bars or restaurants on these dates. Please contact the reception for guidance during these periods.
Check-In & Check-Out
Standard hotel check-in is 14:00. Standard check-out is 12:00. Early check-in and late check-out are subject to availability and may incur additional fees.
Belongings
While we take every precaution, hotel management is not responsible for lost, stolen, or damaged personal belongings. We recommend using hotel safes and exercising care with valuables.
Travel Times
All travel times are approximate and subject to traffic conditions, road works, and weather. Please allow flexibility in your schedule.
Gratuities
Gratuities for guides and drivers are at your discretion. We recommend presenting tips in an envelope at the end of your journey as a token of appreciation.
Dress Code
Light, breathable clothing is recommended for Sri Lanka’s tropical climate. Comfortable walking shoes are essential. At religious sites, dress modestly — covering from knees to elbows.
